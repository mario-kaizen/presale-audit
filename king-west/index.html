Engine 1 Detail
Paid Acquisition — How It Works
The full breakdown of what Kaizen delivers across 16 weeks.

What We Run

  • Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram) — awareness and lead generation campaigns targeting King West locals
  • Lead form ads — low-friction "Register Interest" and "Get Early Access" capture
  • Retargeting — video viewers, page visitors, and form abandoners see follow-up creative
  • Conversion campaigns — Phase 2 onwards, driving intro offer purchases from warm database

Audience Targeting

  • Primary: Women 25–45 within 5km of King West — fitness, wellness, pilates interests
  • Secondary: Lookalike audiences built from existing STRONG member lists
  • Exclusion: Existing STRONG members at other Toronto locations
  • Geo: King West core (1km) + surrounding Toronto neighbourhoods (5km)

Creative Strategy

  • Phase 1: "Coming Soon" curiosity-driven — no pricing, no offers. Build anticipation.
  • Phase 2: Intro offer launch creative — urgency, social proof, class format education
  • Phase 3: Countdown and scarcity — "X spots left", opening week push
  • All phases: Studio buildout content, owner story, STRONG brand video assets

CRM & Automation

  • Lead capture: Meta lead form → Grow CRM → tagged and pipelined automatically
  • Welcome sequence: Immediate SMS + email on registration
  • Nurture drip: Weekly updates — buildout progress, what is STRONG, owner story, community
  • Phase transitions: Automated offer announcements to warm database as phases progress

Why this matters: Paid acquisition is projected to deliver ~960 leads. The organic engine is what takes you beyond that number toward your opening goals. Studios that execute both engines consistently outperform those that rely on paid alone.
