06 — What This Means
The Patterns Across Six Studios

Lead generation works. CPL targets are achievable — four of six studios came in under $10. The ads are doing their job. The constraint isn't at the top of the funnel.

The presale deck targets don't match the data. The deck promises 3,000 leads and 300 members. No studio hit 300 members within the presale window. The two that hit 200 did so by selling memberships directly during presale — a different model.

A $60 cost per member isn't happening at any studio. The closest is Williston Park at $90. The honest range is $90 to $787 per member. The $60 figure only appears if you isolate post-opening spend and ignore what came before it.

There are two different sales models operating under one framework. US studios sold memberships directly during presale. Canadian studios sold intro offers and converted post-opening. Both can work, but they require different strategies, budgets, and targets.

The conversion and retention layer is the real variable. Oakville retains 94% of its converted members. Yorkville retains 34%. The presale can fill the pipeline, but what happens inside the studio determines whether those people stay.

The presale doesn't close memberships — it builds the pipeline that closes memberships in the first six weeks. Canadian presale spend produced 11 memberships combined. Post-opening, those same pipelines produced 192 memberships in six weeks. The investment isn't wasted — it just converts later than the deck implies.

The studios hitting their targets weren't spending more on marketing — they were running two parallel tracks simultaneously: a marketing engine selling intro offers, and a studio sales engine upselling those intro buyers into VIP memberships before the doors opened.

The funnel is: Lead → Intro Offer → VIP Member Upsell. Marketing fills the top. The studio converts from the middle. This strategy lays out both tracks across a single 16-week presale with a 6-week post-opening window. One budget. Two teams. Two measurable outcomes running side by side from day one.

A note on conversion rates — and an honest assumption.

This roadmap targets a 33% intro-to-VIP upsell and 25% post-opening conversion — a combined 50% of intro buyers becoming members. The six-studio audit showed a 19.9% average intro-to-member conversion, with the best studio at 25.4%.

The 33% VIP upsell rate is an assumption we can't validate yet — no audited studio was running this model. We're treating it as a hypothesis to test with the first studio, not a guaranteed outcome. The entire roadmap is Version 1, built from the best data we have. The first presale we run together will tell us whether 33% is realistic, conservative, or aggressive — and we'll adjust from there.

The gap between 20% and 50% is the studio sales track. Without it, the data is clear about what happens:

Phase 1 · Weeks -16 to -10
Build the Audience
7 weeks. Low spend. Maximum reach. No offers yet — just build the pool.
Marketing Track — Kaizen
  • Launch lead generation campaigns — "coming soon" positioning, interest capture
  • Ad spend: $300–$500/week
  • Target: 800–1,200 leads by end of phase
  • CPL target: under $6
  • Build email list, SMS list, and retargeting audiences
  • Set up CRM tracking in Hapana Grow. Every lead tagged by source (ad campaign, organic, referral). Every intro buyer tagged. Every VIP upsell tagged separately. Kaizen manages this — the studio doesn't need to touch it
  • No offers live yet — this phase is about building the pool
  • Creative: construction progress, instructor teasers, location lifestyle
Studio Sales Track — Studio Owner
  • Design the VIP member offer by Week -12. This is the offer intro buyers will be upsold to. It needs to be compelling enough that someone who already bought a $49 intro would upgrade to a membership before they've set foot in the studio
  • Recommended VIP structure: 10–20% below standard membership rate, locked for 12 months. Perks that disappear at opening: priority class booking for life, name on the VIP member wall, free merchandise pack, one guest pass per month for the first 3 months. The perks need to feel exclusive, not discounted
  • Set the VIP cap and make it public. "50 VIP spots" or "75 VIP spots" — a real number that creates real scarcity. This becomes a countdown that drives urgency in Phase 2 and 3
  • Week -16: Owner introduction post. First Instagram post: "I'm [name], and I'm bringing STRONG Pilates to [suburb]. Here's why." This is the owner's face, the owner's story, not a brand template. People commit to people before they commit to studios
  • Weeks -15 to -12: Build-out content series. Weekly posts showing construction progress, floor plan reveals, instructor announcements. Every post ends with a link to the lead capture landing page. This is organic reach — no spend, just consistency
  • Local partnership outreach. Identify 20–30 complementary businesses within 3km: cafes, athleisure stores, wellness practitioners, physios, beauty salons. Approach 20, confirm 5+ by end of Phase 1. These partnerships activate in Phase 2
  • Identify 10–15 local micro-influencers — fitness-adjacent accounts with 1K–10K followers in your suburb. Invite them to the VIP preview class in Phase 3 in exchange for posting about the experience

Retention — This Deserves Its Own Section

The audit data tells a stark story. Oakville converted 104 intro buyers to members and retained 94.2%. Yorkville converted 206 — nearly double — and retained 34%. Two-thirds of Yorkville's converted members have already cancelled. Same brand, same presale model, dramatically different outcomes.

Getting to 210 members means nothing if 140 of them cancel within three months. Retention is not an afterthought — it's the thing that determines whether the presale investment actually pays off for the studio owner.

The retention playbook starts in the first week:

  • Week 1: Welcome message from the owner to every new member. Personal, not automated. "Welcome to the VIP family. Here's my number if you ever need anything."
  • Week 2: Check-in. "How are you finding the classes? Have you tried [instructor name]'s class yet?" The goal is to get them to a second and third visit. Members who attend 3+ classes in the first 2 weeks retain at 2x the rate of those who attend once
  • Week 4: Progress conversation. "You've been coming consistently — how are you feeling? Noticing any changes?" Anchor the experience to results, not just attendance
  • Week 8: Community integration. Invite them to a member event, connect them with other members at their usual class time. Members with social connections in the studio are significantly less likely to cancel
  • Ongoing: Track attendance weekly. Any member who drops from 2+ classes per week to zero for 2 consecutive weeks gets a personal reach-out before they decide to cancel

Version 1 — Built to Be Tested

This is not a final playbook. It's a data-informed starting point — built from six studio audits, designed to be validated by the first presale we run together. The conversion assumptions, the spend curve, and the phase timing are all hypotheses until a real studio proves or disproves them.
